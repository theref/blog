We had a miscarriage and it was awful.

The timing could not have been worse - 2 days before our 12 week scan and 2 days after finding out my best friend is also having a baby.
We were going to be Dad's together.
For 24 hours I imagined that future...our children would grow up together (the due dates were 2 weeks apart).
We'd be able to support each other through pregnancy and parenthood, and experience all these brand new life stages together.

It started a 2am, cramping and bleeding and calls to 111.
At 5am it was time for an ambulance.
Around 7am were in the hospital, vomiting, full of needles, and scared.
We both knew what was happening, but refused to believe it until someone else said it.

Eventually, scans confirmed the worst, but the ordeal wasn't close to over.
More blood, more needles.
Surgery to remove any last hope that this might all be a mistake.

The grief and sleep deprivation were terrible.
I don't know how my partner got through it.
She's incredibly strong.

It's been strange mourning the loss of something that we never really had.
It's the loss of excitement, all of the phone calls to family and friends, the loss of time.

Before this happened, I didn't know anyone who had a miscarriage.
A few have shared their stories since, but not many.
I hope that I can be that person for someone else, and that we can all be more open about the reality of miscarriage, and the fact that it is more common than we think.
